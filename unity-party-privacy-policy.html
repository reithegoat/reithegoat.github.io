Unity Party

Privacy Policy
Last Updated: June 20th, 2022

No one likes to read a complicated wall of text, I don't like writing complicated wall of text. This privacy policy is simplified for the two of us.

Conditions

Agreement

By playing Unity Party via the official binary file, you are agreeing to the privacy policy. If you disagree with the policy, then you are not allowed to play Unity Party via the official binary file.

Policy Change

There will be an in-game notification when the privacy policy change. There will not be a notification for when a future change of the policy will occur.

Exceptions

The privacy policy does not apply to forked, user-modified and/or non-officially compiled versions of Unity Party. Official and trusted binaries of Unity Party is available via the GitHub repository, the Game Jolt page, and the GameBanana page. The Unity Party source code and binaries are licensed under GNU General Public License v3.0.

Services Used

Epic Games Online Services (EOS)

Unity Party uses EOS for peer-to-peer multiplayer networking and matchmaking and to authenticate users for online multiplayer. Unity Party will open the EOS account portal for the user to authenticate into their Epic Games account. The user will have to review the Epic Games Online Services and Epic Games privacy policy for this.

Unity Analytics

Unity Party, when the user is connected to the internet, will send exceptions that occur during runtime and crash reports to Rei the Goat for analyzing. Crash reports and exception reports contains stack traces and system information (ex, operating system, RAM amount, graphics card).

Mirror Networking

Mirror Networking is the multiplayer framework behind the online multiplayer mode. Mirror Networking uses EOS to transport and send data between online connected and authenticated players. Data transmitted includes only critical game and user data, no personal information is gained or transmitted to other connected players.
